Projects

Frozen Dessert from Hemp Milk

This project focused on developing a plant-based frozen dessert using hemp milk as an alternative to dairy. Hemp milk was chosen for its high-quality protein, balanced fatty acid profile, and natural bioactive compounds. The study emphasized achieving a creamy mouthfeel and appealing sensory properties while remaining lactose-free and vegan-friendly. Various stabilizers and sweeteners were tested to optimize texture, melting resistance, and consumer acceptability. Results showed that hemp milk could serve as a viable base for frozen desserts, contributing to the growing demand for sustainable and functional non-dairy foods.

Credit Seminar: Oleogels as Novel Fat Replacers in Meat

This seminar examined the potential of oleogels—structured vegetable oil systems—as healthier replacements for saturated animal fats in meat products. Oleogels are formed by trapping liquid oils within a thermo-reversible gel network using oleogelators like waxes, monoglycerides, and phytosterols. My work reviewed various approaches to oleogel formation, including solvent exchange and foam templating, and assessed their functionality in meat systems such as patties, burgers, and sausages. Studies highlighted oleogels’ ability to improve nutritional profiles, reduce cooking loss, and maintain structural stability, while also acting as carriers for bioactive compounds. However, challenges remain in consumer acceptability and large-scale application, pointing to future research needs. This seminar deepened my understanding of lipid structuring, functional food innovation, and sustainable fat alternatives.
